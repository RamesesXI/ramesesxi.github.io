This is a narrative of my journey into game development, providing context and background on how I embarked on this path.

Lifelong Gaming Enthusiast:

Gaming has been a passion of mine since the age of four, starting with simple PC games on the family computer, evolving into online flash games, PlayStation 2, Game Boy, PSP, mobile games, and eventually, a vast array of PC games (often cracked versions, which I'm not particularly proud of).


My quest for the next obsession led me to explore and enjoy almost every game genre, from big studio productions to indie gems. It was the latter—creative games like Don't Starve, Stardew Valley, and Battlerite—that ignited my desire to create games myself.


Competitive games also held a significant allure, and I achieved notable ranks through mastering fundamentals and mechanics in games like League of Legends, Overwatch, and Age of Empires 4.

From Math Whiz to Game Dev Aspirant:

My academic inclination towards mathematics, where problem-solving felt like a game, laid the foundation for my interest in coding within Unity. Translating mathematical concepts into tangible gameplay mechanics was a revelation, solidifying game development as my chosen career path.


An intensive C#/Unity bootcamp, coupled with solo game projects and a computer science degree, equipped me with a robust skillset. However, initial job applications to game dev companies were unsuccessful, leading to a period of introspection and learning about the importance of professional presentation and market-demanded skills.

The Solo Development Rollercoaster:

Resentful, I embarked on a journey of solo development, initially creating smaller games to familiarize myself with the process. My ambition was to create a hit multiplayer turn-based combat game with a dedicated following, a goal I believed in from the outset. This endeavor, however, led to creative blocks, scrapped projects, and a loss of confidence in my abilities as a game developer.


Despite my skillset, I realized that my motivations were not solely career and financial security driven. I desired to create epic, life-changing games independently, setting unrealistic expectations that eventually led to disillusionment.

A Maturing Perspective and Collaborative Endeavors:

A brief hiatus from game dev to explore skills like SQL, Python, and Stable Diffusion led to a collaboration with an artist on a new game idea. This collaborative experience proved transformative, offering a fresh perspective on game development and replacing my previous arrogance with grounded, realistic thinking.


With a renewed sense of purpose, I now see working full-time for a reputable company as the next step in my journey. Contributing my skills to a larger project while learning and growing as a developer aligns with my evolving aspirations in the dynamic world of game development.
